What is GearHub?

The idea for GearHub was born when I saw a Facebook post about a friend's band having their equipment stolen from their van while on tour. The damage? Nearly $30,000 worth of equipment was stolen.

Unfortunately, theft is very common in the music scene. Beyond that, music equipment is extremely expensive and often irreplacable. Both the authorities and their insurance agency requested pictures and serial numbers of all of the equipment that was stolen. Without this vital information, it would be impossible to recover their missing gear.

The idea behind GearHub is an inventory management solutions for amateur and professional musicians or studio engineers. Users can add information and photos of their instruments and other pro audio gear and find an integrated view of their home or professional studio. This important information is vital in the event of theft, loss or other catastrophe.

As a musician and amateur studio engineer, I understand that fellow musicians are always in pursuit of the perfect sound. Beyond merely functioning as inventory control, GearHub utilizes the Reverb API to query and populate twelve-month average ask and final sale prices for equipment that has been added to our database. This information helps users visualize price trends on their equipment and identify the best way to fund their next big purchase

In the future, I also intend to incorporate a social media element that will allow musicians and engineers to collaborate on projects and to buy, sell and trade equipment.
