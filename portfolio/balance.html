What is Balance?

For my final/group project during my last weeks at DevMountain, I pitched the idea of creating a personal dashboard to assist users with scheduling events, tracking ideas, visualizing progress towards their goals, and impoving productivity. Inspired by everyday struggles with time management, organization, planning, and focus, I wanted to create an intuitive solution for bringing these elements into balance. After three weeks, our small team of four presented the final product: Balance.

Our design was influced by the popular Momentum new-tab extension for Chrome. For developers and other professionals who spend time behind the screen, the browser home page is an uninvasive, but frequently viewed destination. Each time you open a new tab or browser window, you have a holistic view of your upcoming events, tools to help you track your thoughts, your to-dos, your daily habit and goals, and much more. We hope that this site inspires balance and productivity in your day-to-day life!

This project was my first opportunity to work with other developers in a collaborative space. It helped me learn a lot about myself in a team dynamic, how to debug other developers' code, and develop valuable and healthy habits for version control when working with a team.

Please feel free to make an account and bring some Balance into your life!

Screenshots

Landing


From the landing page, a user can register a new account. Information is encrypted and stored in our database to protect users from potential breaches.

Dashboard

The dashboard home screen shows a welcome message, the current time and date, as well as upcoming events. From here, a user can access current weather (and seven-day forecast) in the upper-right. When activated, a Focus Timer will populate in the lower-right to help the user focus on their tasks. On the left, a nav menu will allow users to access the habit tracker, calendar, notepad, to-do list, settings, timer and theme customization features.

Calendar

One of the motivations behind creating this application was to develop a simple, easy-to-use interface that offers a myriad of useful tools. Displayed here is the calendar, which allows a user to add event information. The user specifies a name, date, time, importance level and description. "HI" important events are displayed on the home page a week in advance while "LO" important events are displayed 24 hours before the event.
